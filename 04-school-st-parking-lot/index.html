Property Owner Written Approval

Public Event License (CitizenServe)
$50–$200 4–6 Weeks
▼
Required for events on private property open to the public or above size thresholds. Submit at least 13 days before the next City Council meeting — plan for 4–6 weeks total lead time. Apply via Somerville CitizenServe portal under "Public Event/Special Alcohol License" even if no alcohol is planned.

Fire Prevention Bureau Permit
$75–$300 2–4 Weeks

Generator / Electrical Permit
$100–$300 2–3 Weeks

7
Noise Ordinance Compliance
$0 (violations $100–$300) Hard Cutoff: 10pm
▼
No permit required, but Spring Hill is a mixed residential neighborhood — violations are costly and reputation-damaging. MassDEP rule: ambient +10 dB at property line is a violation. General limits: 70 dB daytime, 50 dB after 11pm. Recommend ending all amplified music by 10pm sharp.
Mitigation: Sound barriers (hay bales, acoustic drapes), aim speakers inward, hire a sound engineer with a calibrated dB meter.
🔊 End amplified sound by 10pm · Hire sound engineer with dB meter
8
Event Liability Insurance ($1M/$2M)
$150–$500 Before Permit Submission
▼
Somerville requires general liability insurance of $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate for public events. The property owner will likely require additional insured status. Obtain online from NEXT Insurance, Markel, or similar specialty providers.
Action: Get event insurance quote online; add City of Somerville AND lot owner as additional insureds.
