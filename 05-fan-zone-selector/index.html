Scoring Methodology

Transparent Scoring Framework

Every site scored on 6 criteria using objective infrastructure data from Cyvl's Somerville dataset. Weights reflect FIFA's ADA mandate and public safety requirements for large outdoor events.

25%
ADA Accessibility

Ramp count, push buttons, curb cuts, sidewalk continuity, flashing beacons. Weighted highest because FIFA fan zones must comply with ADA accessible routes and viewing areas. Failure here is a legal disqualifier for grant funding.

20%
Safety Infrastructure

Traffic signal count, CCTV coverage, standalone pedestrian signals. Large public gatherings require robust traffic management and security infrastructure — ranked second after ADA compliance.

15%
Pavement Quality

PCI average score plus penalties for Failed/Serious segments within 400m. Poor pavement directly compromises ADA access and crowd safety — wheelchair users and standing crowds are both impacted by degraded surfaces.

15%
Event Amenities

Tree count for natural shade, streetlight count for night events, bike rack access, and food/beverage density. June/July Massachusetts events require sun protection. Streetlights enable evening matches.

15%
Crowd Capacity

Usable open area × 0.4 usable fraction × 1 person/0.56 sqm (ADA standing density). Tiers: 2,000+ people (15 pts), 1,000–1,999 (11 pts), 500–999 (7 pts), under 500 (4 pts).

10%
Transit Access

Direct T station (8 pts), nearby T walk (5 pts), bus-only (2 pts). Good transit reduces parking demand and enables larger attendance. Weighted lowest — Somerville's dense network means most sites have some access.

Why ADA Is Weighted Highest

FIFA fan festival requirements mandate fully accessible venues under federal ADA law. For public events on public land receiving state grant funding, ADA compliance is not optional — it is a legal prerequisite. A site with excellent pavement but inadequate ramp counts or degraded accessible routes cannot receive grant funding and exposes the city to litigation. Cyvl's infrastructure data makes ADA compliance quantifiable before a dollar of grant money is committed.

Key Findings

What Cyvl Found That You Can't See From a Map

Infrastructure intelligence reveals crowd safety risks, logistics constraints, and ADA compliance gaps invisible to traditional site surveys.

1

Teele Square: 9 Failed Pavement Segments — Immediate Safety Disqualifier

Cyvl's pavement inspection data reveals 9 segments with PCI scores below 10 (Failed) within 400m of Teele Square — the most dangerous road condition classification. Combined with 32 Serious segments and 200 total poor-or-worse inspections (highest of all sites), Teele Square cannot safely host large crowds. A tripping hazard or inaccessible route at a FIFA event constitutes an ADA violation and creates significant municipal liability. This risk is invisible without Cyvl data.

2

Assembly Row: Only 2 Utility Poles — Uniquely Suited for Large-Screen Staging

Most fan zones require large LED screens (typically 10m×6m or larger) for match broadcasts. Utility poles create sightline obstructions, safety clearance zones, and permitting complications. Assembly Row's underground utilities (a product of its post-2013 development) leave only 2 poles detected within 400m — compared to Magoun Square's 28 and Davis Square's 26. This single data point saves event logistics teams weeks of site survey work and city coordination.

3

Union Square's New Plaza Scores High, But 127 Poor Inspections on Approach Streets

Union Square's post-2023 plaza redesign creates an excellent central gathering space — but the streets fans walk to reach it (Charlestown Street with Serious segments PCI 11–22, Allen Street with Very Poor PCI 25–39, Windsor Place with Very Poor PCI 38–40) represent a significant crowd routing challenge. Cyvl's 400m radius analysis revealed this disconnect: the destination is good, the journey there is not. Any grant application for Union Square must include pavement remediation funding for approach corridors.

4

ADA Ramp Counts Vary 70% Across Sites — Critical for FIFA's Accessibility Mandate

East Somerville leads with 22 ramps detected within 400m; Magoun Square has only 13 — a 70% difference in accessible infrastructure density. FIFA fan festival specifications require fully accessible routes, companion seating in all viewing zones, and accessible sanitation. Cyvl's above-ground asset data maps exactly where ramps exist and where gaps remain, enabling targeted capital investment before grant submission rather than after the fact.

5

Assembly Row's CCTV Density (3 Cameras) vs. Two Sites with Zero Coverage

Security screening is mandatory at FIFA fan festivals. While temporary security infrastructure can be deployed, existing CCTV provides continuous crowd monitoring, emergency response coordination, and post-event incident review. Assembly Row's 3 CCTV cameras — combined with its adjacent hotel security infrastructure — create a significantly stronger baseline security environment. Union Square and East Somerville have zero CCTV within 400m, meaning full security infrastructure costs would be borne by the event budget.
