Step-by-Step Guide

8-Step Permit Process

File all permits simultaneously — not sequentially. Total lead time: 6–8 weeks. All permits must be in hand before event day.

1
Special Event License
MOCAL $50–$200 Apply 30+ days out
▾
Requirements
  • Completed application signed by Boston Police District D-4 Captain
  • Site plan / event layout diagram
  • Security plan
  • Certificate of insurance ($1M+ general liability)
  • Letter from property owner / landlord if on private property
📧 MOCAL@boston.gov  |  City Hall Room 809
🌐 boston.gov/departments/consumer-affairs-and-licensing
This is your anchor permit — everything else flows from it. File this first. Small commercial events qualify for Tier 1 (minimal city resources).
2
ISD Temporary Food Service Permit
ISD Health Division $30 day 1, $5/day after Apply 30+ days out
▾
Requirements
  • Completed temporary food establishment application
  • Proof of certified food manager (or current enrollment in certification)
  • Food handling procedures
  • Equipment and setup plan
📍 1010 Massachusetts Ave, 4th Floor
📞 (617) 635-5326
Valid for up to 14 consecutive days — if running multiple Open Newbury Sundays, a multi-day permit saves money and paperwork.
3
One-Day Alcohol License (if serving)
Boston Licensing Board $75–$150/day Apply 3–4 weeks out
▾
Requirements
  • Application signed by Police Captain in event district (D-4)
  • ABCC (Alcoholic Beverages Control Commission) application
  • Certificate of Inspection from ISD
  • Fire Assembly Permit (for 49+ capacity events)
  • Property owner/landlord permission letter
  • Site plan showing alcohol service area and all egresses
  • Security plan with staffing ratios
  • Must purchase alcohol from ABCC-approved wholesaler only
📧 Alyssa.Tobin@boston.gov (CC: LicensingBoard@boston.gov)
🌐 boston.gov/licensing-board
Malt/wine: $75/day. All-alcohol: $150/day. Email is fastest — board reviews weekly.
4
Outdoor Dining / Sidewalk Cafe Permit (if on public sidewalk)
Public Works + Licensing $199–$399/month Apply before May 1
▾
Requirements
  • Business must have existing food service license
  • ADA-compliant layout — maintain 5-foot clear pedestrian passage at all times
  • Application via boston.gov portal
  • Bollards or delineators may be required for boundary marking
  • Liquor license required to serve alcohol in outdoor dining area
📧 outdoordining@boston.gov
Season runs May 1 – October 31
For a pure pop-up (not attached to an existing licensed business), use the Special Event path instead of this permit. The seasonal permit is for brick-and-mortar operators expanding outdoors.
5
Tent / Temporary Structure Permit
Boston Fire Prevention Varies File with other permits
▾
Requirements
  • Required for any tent cluster larger than 4 × 10×10 with sides attached
  • Key exception: 4 or fewer 10×10 tents touching without sides = NO permit needed
  • Larger aggregations require permit
  • Stamped engineer/architect approval if capacity exceeds 199 people
🌐 boston.gov/fire-prevention
Smart move: use 4 × 10×10 open-sided tents. No permit required, and you get 400 sq ft of covered space free of the fire permitting process.
6
Generator Permit
Fire Prevention / ISD $50–$100 File with other permits
▾
Requirements
  • Generator permit application
  • Fuel storage documentation
  • Location plan showing generator placement relative to tents and structures
No public power outlets on Newbury Street sidewalks. Generator or battery power pack required for any powered equipment — POS systems, lighting, music.
7
One-Time Entertainment License (if live music)
Entertainment Licensing / MOCAL Varies Apply 2+ weeks out
▾
Requirements
  • Application with signed police captain approval
  • Event details, performance times, security plan
  • May require noise management plan for amplified sound
📧 MOCAL@boston.gov
🌐 boston.gov/entertainment-licensing
The Google Store Live Music event (Sep 2025) at 153 Newbury used exactly this permit. Precedent is strong for music activations on Open Newbury Sundays.
8
Transient Vendor License (MA State) (if selling goods)
MA Secretary of State State fee varies Apply 2–3 weeks out
▾
Requirements
  • Required if selling retail goods from a temporary location
  • Does NOT apply to established brick-and-mortar businesses doing an outdoor extension
🌐 mass.gov/how-to/transient-vendor
If you already have a Boston business license at a permanent address and are extending outdoors, skip this. Applies primarily to new pop-ups without an existing storefront.

Open Newbury Hack
On Open Newbury Sundays, the city closes all 8 blocks to cars. Public sidewalk space is effectively free — the only cost is your special event permit ($50–$200), not the monthly outdoor dining fee ($199–$399/mo).
