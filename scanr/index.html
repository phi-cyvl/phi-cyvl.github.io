The idea

What if every score carried its own confidence?

Built from the scan you already paid for. No new fieldwork. No hand-measured ground truth. Each score is checked against the evidence beneath it and against how long ago the road was driven.

Verified · evidence backs the score Review · signals are mixed Rescan · evidence disagrees, or the scan is stale
What it's worth

Rescan what moved. Skip what didn't.

Confidence decays as the world changes under a scan. That decay is a map of where to look next. Send crews to the miles that carry the uncertainty, not the whole network, for a fraction of the cost, and keep the years between full scans defensible. Forward-looking deterioration is the #1 thing customers ask us for, and the timing is now: Atlanta wants to scan monthly, with ten Year-2 rescans landing in the next 120 days, six of them pre-renewal.

Sources: Time Machine recap (deterioration = top critical gap; 57% would use it today); Framing: Time Machine, Jul 2026 (Atlanta monthly; 10 second scans in 120 days, all ARR; priority usage → retention → NRR).

How the confidence is computed

Two questions, asked of every score.

A score can't grade its own homework. So each one is checked against evidence it never sees at scoring time — no ground truth, no peek at next year.

1. Does it match the road under it? Compare the score to the distress the sensors actually measured on that segment. A high score sitting on heavy cracking is a disagreement.
2. Does it match its neighbors? Compare the score to nearby segments on the same street. A lone outlier, or a segment with too little coverage, is suspect.
Big disagreement on either one → low confidence → rescan first. The two checks combine into one 0–1 score per segment. That is exactly what colors the map above.

This is a self-supervised proxy: honest, cheap, and computed from the one scan alone. The fuller version we're building scores each prediction with a strictly proper rule (the Brier score, which can't be lowered by sounding over-sure) and adds a check against last year — the same idea behind our ground-truth-free research.

Demo method: spatial-consistency + score-vs-distress agreement per segment (litmus-demo/confidence_demo.py). Proper-scoring foundation: Gneiting & Raftery 2007; priormap research (pre-registered).

A few years out · the vision

Let the rescanning drive itself.

The confidence layer decides which miles to rescan. The research question: could the rescanning decide itself? We trained a swarm to do it — and its training reward is the same ground-truth-free audit that scores each road's confidence. No answer key is used: not for the confidence, not for the training.
