Prioritized Action Plan

Recommendations ordered by urgency. Each is tied directly to findings from Cyvl infrastructure data and Vision Zero crash records.

1

Emergency Repair — McGrath Highway PCI 23.55

Patch segment 7e2445e1 (Serious-rated structural failure) before any event planning places this route in service. Address the two adjacent Very Poor segments (PCI 26.31, 32.69). Install barriers at construction zone overlap. Cost estimated $15-40K per block — a fraction of the $76M safety allocation.

Before May 2026
2

Commission Broadway Re-Scan + Boston Corridor Scan

Broadway has 78% unscored segments — cannot be used in evacuation planning without data. Additionally, Congress St, Atlantic Ave, Summer St, and Cambridge St (Boston jurisdiction) need Cyvl scanning. These are the primary City Hall Plaza dispersal routes with zero current PCI data.

Jan-Mar 2026
3

ADA Ramp Repair — Features 1196 & 1163

Replace both non-compliant ramps with ADA-compliant units including truncated domes, wing flares, and minimum 36-inch width. Install temporary surface bridging over 64ft (Highland Ave) and 128ft (Somerville Ave) no-sidewalk gaps. Per ADA Title II, every accessible space requires at least one accessible egress path.

Mar-May 2026
4

Designate & Sign 3 Primary Evacuation Corridors

Officially designate: (1) Cambridge St to Highland Ave to Davis Square Red Line, (2) Congress St to South Station, (3) Tremont St to Boston Common dispersal. Install emergency evacuation signage, pre-position crowd managers at identified utility pole pinch points, and establish vehicle exclusion zones. At 150 persons/min per corridor: 3 corridors handle 27,000 persons/hour — full 50K dispersal requires ~2 hours minimum with staged release.

Apr-May 2026
5

Pre-Event Distress Remediation — Top 15-20 Segments

Address high-severity distresses on Highland and Somerville Ave: 591 sq ft weathering (Highland), 610 sq ft weathering (Somerville Ave), 179 sq ft alligator cracking, rutting and bumps/sags on PCI 40.7 segment. These are fall hazards for fast-moving crowds — especially critical for nighttime events when pavement damage is harder to see.

May 2026
6

Deploy Cyvl Continuous Monitoring During Event Period

Run Cyvl scanning vehicles on designated evacuation corridors weekly during the June 2026 event period to detect rapid pavement deterioration, new construction activity, and infrastructure changes. Real-time condition data enables emergency rerouting if a corridor degrades between matches.
